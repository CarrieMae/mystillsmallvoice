About Me

Welcome! I’m Carrie—a nurse, former Mormon, mom, and late-diagnosis ADHD advocate. This space is my journey from the fast-paced OR to the slow, reflective work of rediscovering my beliefs and finding my voice. Also, yes, this is an unprofessional photo that I happen to like, so judgments are allowed—just not to my face!

From the OR to Honest Reflections
As an OR nurse, humor is our survival tool, and my team is family—one googly-eye prank at a time! I share OR stories to give a glimpse into the laughter and camaraderie that keeps us grounded in a high-stakes environment.

Life Beyond Belief
After 31 years in the Mormon faith, leaving was one of my hardest decisions, but it opened up my world. Here, I share reflections on questioning, healing, and trusting myself outside high-demand religion, hoping to offer comfort and empowerment.

Navigating Neurodivergence
A late ADHD diagnosis was like finding a missing piece of myself. I know how confusing those first steps can be, so I share support, encouragement, and practical life hacks that helped me—and might help you, too.

I also provide resources for Arizona parents navigating autism diagnoses for their children, offering guidance I wish I’d had. Thank you for joining me in this mix of OR stories, personal insights, and practical advice. I hope you’ll find laughter, understanding, and maybe a fresh perspective along the way.
